Heike Crab

March 28, 2025

Heike Crab, Heikeopsis japonica, is a species of crab native to Japan, with a shell that bears a pattern resembling a human face – an example of pareidolia – which is interpreted to be the face of an angry samurai, hence the nickname samurai crab. The crabs are named after the powerful Taira clan, which dominated medieval Japan. It is believed that these crabs are reincarnations of the Heike warriors defeated at the naval Battle of Dan-no-ura.
